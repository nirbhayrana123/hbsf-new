    OUR JOURNEY

    Early 2000s
    2005–2009
    2010–2014
    2015–2018
    2019–2022
    2022 and Onwards
    Early 2000s
    Early 2000s

    With a simple intention to give back, the early years focused on staff welfare initiatives and personal acts of giving, engaging employees and their families in the journey.

    2005–2009
    2005–2009

    Support grew from individuals to local organizations in education, healthcare, arts and culture, guided by on-ground needs and new learnings.

    2010–2014
    2010–2014

    With guidance from philanthropic advisors, giving became more collaborative. Networks expanded and engagement within the sector grew deeper.

    2015–2019
    2015–2018

    Engaging with communities and organizations deepened our understanding, strengthened due diligence, revealed interconnections, and expanded our work to new geographies.

    2019–2022
    2019–2022

    Drawing on our experience and learnings, we quickly adapted during the pandemic supporting urgent needs from healthcare and food to digital access, mental health, and livelihoods.

    2022 and Onwards
    2022 and Onwards

    Through deeper work with institutions and strong nonprofit partners across India, the foundation is strengthening its investments in people, practices, and systems to contribute to a more sustainable social sector.

    OUR GIVING PHILOSOPHY

    We believe giving is both heartfelt and a discipline. At HBSF, we bring structure to compassion, ensuring that our support is deeply considered.

    GUIDING PRINCIPLES

    Sahayak
    Partnering with Purpose

    We see ourselves as partners—supporting, enabling, and co-creating solutions with the people we work. Our approach is patient and long-term, built on trust and shared purpose.

    Samata
    Parity with All

    We believe change happens when everyone stands on equal footing. We focus on creating opportunity, and on building lasting independence.

    Sarthakta
    Meaningful Impact

    We commit to goal-oriented work that is practical and purposeful, ensuring every action is rooted in relevant change. 

    Sanidhya
    Engaged and Present

    We are there in person, in practice, and in spirit. We take the time to listen, to understand, and to walk with communities as they shape their own futures.

    Founding Members

    Harish Shah

    A first-generation entrepreneur and philanthropist, Harish chairs HBSF and leads Signet Capital, focusing on strategy, governance, and institution-building across education, healthcare, and heritage. He previously founded Signet Excipients.

    Bina Shah

    Bina is Executive Director at HBSF. leading strategy and systems. With over 30 years experience in human resource and administration at Signet Excipients, she brings a values led, people first approach to the foundation.

    Amira Shah Chhabra

    Amira is CEO & Executive Director at HBSE. With a background in management and development consulting, she leads operations and partnerships-driven by a deep personal commitment to systemic, long term change.

    Robin Chhabra

    Robin, Founder & CEO of Dextrus, brings deep architectural experience across sectors. As Executive Director at HBSF, he focuses on public infrastructure projects, especially in healthcare, with a design thinking approach.

    Anushka Shah

    Anushka is the Founder & CEO of Civic Studios. With a background in media, data science, and social justice, she advises HBSF on partnerships advancing behavioural change and media driven community engagement.

    John Firth

    John is Director of Global Health & Development at Rethink Priorities. He advises HBSF on economic development and higher education, drawing on research experience from MIT, J-PAL, Notre Dame, and Oxford.
